Kuruvi

Kuruvi is a 2008 Tamil-language action-comedy film directed by Dharani, starring Vijay and Trisha Krishnan in the lead roles, with Suman, Vivek, and Ashish Vidyarthi in supporting roles. The film is known for its high-octane action sequences, comedy, and Vijay's energetic performance.

The story revolves around Vetrivel (Vijay), a happy-go-lucky young man who works as a car racer and a courier, often referred to as a "Kuruvi" (meaning "sparrow" in Tamil), someone who smuggles goods. Vetrivel's father, Singamuthu (Manivannan), has mysteriously disappeared after a trip to Malaysia. Vetrivel learns that his father was cheated by a wealthy and powerful man named Koccha (Suman), who is involved in illegal mining activities and smuggling.

Determined to find out the truth and retrieve his family's lost fortune, Vetrivel travels to Malaysia, where he comes across Koccha and his brother Konda Reddy (Ashish Vidyarthi). In the process, he meets and eventually falls in love with Devi (Trisha), who happens to be Koccha's sister. Unbeknownst to Devi, Vetrivel is trying to bring down her brother's criminal empire.

The film follows Vetrivel as he navigates through a series of challenges, including intense chases, fights, and the development of his romantic relationship with Devi. The climax builds up to a confrontation between Vetrivel and Koccha, where Vetrivel uses his wit and courage to outsmart the villains and restore justice.

"Kuruvi" is known for its vibrant music composed by Vidyasagar and its mass appeal, particularly due to Vijay's charismatic performance. Despite mixed reviews from critics, the film was a commercial success and remains popular among fans of Tamil cinema.
